Background and related projects

National Plan for Open Science

The second national plan makes open science an integral part of the missions of researchers and lectures and sets a target for 100% of publications to be open access by 2030. It is based on the national policy on data, Algorithms and Source Codes promoted by the Prime Minister, which aims to facilitate researchers' access to public data by publishing the State's commitment charter to accelerate the processing of researchers' requests.

Among the measures highlighted (translated from French):

Translate French researchers' publications to promote the dissemination of scientific knowledge: research on climate, biodiversity and health, which is published in English, will be translated to make it accessible to the general public, and research published in French by historians or sociologists will be translated to increase their impact internationally.

Access the pdf.

See also Report by the "Translations and open science" working group (in French).

Helsinki Initiative on Multilingualism in Scholarly Communication

The signatories of the Helsinki Initiative on Multilingualism in Scholarly Communication support the following recommendations to be adopted by policy-makers, leaders, universities, research institutions, research funders, libraries, and researchers:

  1. Support dissemination of research results for the full benefit of society.
  2. Protect national infrastructures for publishing locally relevant research.
  3. Promote language diversity in research assessment, evaluation, and funding systems.

Assocation for Computational Linguistics (ACL) 60-60 Globalization via Localization

The ACL 2022 Diversity and Inclusion Special Initiative, 60-60, is a year long challenge, with the theme of Globalisation via localisation. The initiative aims to translate and create all conference materials (text papers, speech, and provide sign language)— ACL anthology content — into 60 languages. The goal is democratisation of scientific content for computational linguistics. Hopefully with genuine scientific outreach in many languages, we start to unlock more native talent/creativity/ scientific innovation allowing for more voices to be heard in their native tongues transcending language barriers. With this effort they hope to lower the ingrained language bias in the scientific landscape.
