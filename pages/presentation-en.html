Project presentation

The MaTOS (Machine Translation for Open Science) project aims to develop new methods for the machine translation (MT) of full scientific documents, as well as automatic metrics to evaluate the quality of the translations produced. Our main use case is the translation of scientific articles between French and English, for which linguistic resources can be exploited to obtain more reliable translations, both for publication purposes, for gisting and text mining. However, efforts to improve the MT of complete documents are hampered by the inability of existing automatic metrics to detect weaknesses in systems and to identify the best ways to remedy them. The MaTOS project aims to address both of these issues. This project is part of a movement to automate the processing of scientific articles. The field of MT is no exception to this trend, particularly in the biomedical field. The applications are numerous: text mining, bibliometric analysis, the automatic detection of plagiarism and of articles reporting false conclusions, etc. We wish to take advantage of the results of this work, but also to contribute in many ways:

  1. by developing new open resources for specialised MT;
  2. by improving, through the study of terminological variations, the description of textual coherence markers for scientific articles;
  3. by studying new methods of multilingual processing for these documents;
  4. by proposing metrics dedicated to the measurement of progress for this type of task. The final result, through improved translation, will faciltate the circulation and dissemination of scientific knowledge.
